IT INFRASTRUCTURE AND NETWORK TECHNOLOGIES

During our second year in college, we conducted a network infrastructure and analysis project for BigFish Internet Café as part of our subject, IT Infrastructure and Network Technologies. The project focused on studying the café’s existing network setup, including its topology, network devices, cabling, traffic patterns, and overall performance. We analyzed how the internet café managed its operations using routers, switches, servers, and personal computers connected through a Local Area Network (LAN). As part of the study, we used Cisco Packet Tracer to simulate and evaluate the café’s network performance, identify issues such as latency and traffic congestion, and propose an improved network design. Our proposed solution included upgrading network components, adding additional servers and switches, and implementing a hybrid topology to improve bandwidth and data transmission efficiency. Through this project, we gained practical knowledge in network design, troubleshooting, simulation, and infrastructure planning while developing our teamwork, technical, and analytical skills in solving real-world networking problems.
