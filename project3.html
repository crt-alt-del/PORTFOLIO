Data Structure and Algorithm

During our second year in college, we conducted a study entitled *“Assessment of the Radix Sort Algorithm.”* The study focused on understanding how the Radix Sort algorithm works and evaluating its efficiency in handling sorting operations involving large sets of data. We explored the algorithm’s time complexity, performance, strengths, and limitations while comparing it with other sorting algorithms such as Merge Sort, Quick Sort, and Heap Sort. As part of the project, we applied the algorithm to a real-world simulation involving employee details and phone number sorting to demonstrate its practical applications. Through this study, we gained deeper knowledge about data structures and algorithms, particularly in analyzing sorting techniques and their effectiveness in different scenarios. The project also enhanced our analytical thinking, programming skills, and research abilities, while giving us practical experience in solving real-world computational problems using algorithmic approaches.
